SIBERIAN HUSKY

The Siberian husky is a graceful, athletic dog with high endurance and an eagerness to work. This friendly dog breed, which originated in Northeast Asia as a sled dog, has a vivacious and mischievous personality. Though these are high-energy and sometimes intense dogs, huskies can be affectionate and gentle when given proper care. They were brought to America through Alaska.

What Is A Siberian Husky Behaviour Like?

Siberian Husky does not display the possessive qualities of the guard dog, nor are they overly suspicious of strangers or aggressive with other dogs.Indeed, the free-spirited Siberian Husky is usually good-natured with everyone.They are playful, athletic, agile, and light on their feet. They love the great outdoors and requires vigorous exercise, especially in cool weather. They should be taken running, hiking, and/or biking every day, always on-leash, for he is independent and born to run.Teaching them to pull carts and sleds gives him a purpose in life. Without such exercise, mental activity, and lots of companionship, they can be incredibly, massively destructive.Most Siberian Huskies are sociable with other dogs, but they have a very high prey drive and may destroy cats if not raised with them. Siberians should not be kept around rabbits, ferrets, or birds. Fencing must be high and secure, for they can be an escape artist, and once loose, they may run deer and molest livestock.Training is a challenge because they are strong-willed.Siberian Husky is inclined to use his intelligence in clever ways that suit their own purposes. Digging and howling are favorite pastimes.



Are Siberian Huskies Easy To Train?

Huskies are difficult to train due to their mix of intelligence and independence. They are intelligent dogs, so they understand very quickly what you teach them. But their independence means they may choose to ignore you.While Huskies can be difficult to train, they are very trainable. With the right training approach, you can train a Husky to be well behaved and follow your commands.Huskies being untrainable is a myth that reflects the poor training practices often shared around.If a Husky owner doesn’t adjust their training method to account for a Husky’s independence, they’re going to have a tough time.

What Are The Pros And Cons Of Having A Siberian Husky?

Pros

  • The Siberian husky is a friendly and gentle breed of dog if taken care of properly. They are also very playful to whoever handles them.
  • A Siberian husky has a lot of stamina which makes them a very active dog.
  • Unlike other dog breeds that give a bad odor even after few days after giving them a good bath, a Siberian husky does not stink or give off a foul odor.
  • One of the best advantage of having an Arctic breed, the husky as a pet is that they are originally bred to perform on a cold weather with minimal food in mind.

Cons

  • The Siberian huskies are masters of escape. Untrained huskies will break out on even the smallest hole that they can squeeze into.
  • While the Siberian husky has a lot of stamina that let them play and stay active all the time, this can be a disadvantage to some people especially if that person doesn’t want to go outside a lot and exercise.
  • Siberian huskies shed a lot of furs per year. They shed twice a year usually before summer and before winter. If cleaning shed furs is not your thing, then owning a Siberian husky is a big no for you.

Some Interesting Facts About Siberian Husky

The husky was originally bred by the Chukchi people of northeast Siberia.They are one of the oldest dog breeds.

Huskies Have a Lot of Energy—and Make Great Jogging Buddies

They rarely bark. Huskies mainly communicate through howling and whining.

Hygiene-wise, they are pretty low-maintenance. They only need a few baths a year (depending on the climate) and weekly brushing.

Siberian Huskies are very friendly with strangers.
